MultiCard

The Swiss Army Knife of Card Components

MultiCards are card components that use subcards to add extra information, user interactivity, and more.

The idea for MultiCard originally stemmed from the idea of making a digital business card. This use of MultiCard demonstrates an example of its original purpose.

Click on sections of the card to view its functionality.

Of course, sliding subcards can occur for different types of events aside from clicks. Here's an example of a possible onHover card that can be used for minimalist image galleries.

Different types of templates are planned for Multicards. As of right now, cards can have header titles if wanted.
Here is an example of a MultiCard with header used to make trivia cards that display hints and answers.

Now, you might be thinking, 'I get it, it's just a card component with drawers'. However, these examples only scratch the surface in terms of the creative layouts you can use these components in.

MultiCards use a linked structure where each MultiCard points to a subcard in each of its four directions: up, down, left, and right. But, each subcard is also a MultiCard! That means they can have their own subcards.
